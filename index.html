Catalina
Coming September 2017 from MCD, a new imprint from Farrar, Straus and Giroux

Elsa Fisher is adrift. She has just been fired from MoMA on the heels of an affair with her married boss and is returning to Los Angeles with a hefty severance package and a fistful of pills to numb the pain. Her abandoned crew of college friends (childhood friend Charlotte, her wayward husband Jared, and Elsa’s ex-husband Robby) receive her with open arms and a plan to celebrate their reunion on a booze-soaked sailing trip to Catalina Island.

Elsa’s friends believe she’s on vacation, but her carefully maintained façade starts to crack on Catalina, barely concealing her loneliness and rage, not to mention a snowballing addiction. It doesn’t help that her friends have secrets of their own—underneath all the forced comradery lurks painful memories and strained ties—and Elsa’s determined unraveling threatens to drag everyone down with her.

Liska Jacobs holds an MFA from the University of California Riverside Palm Desert. Her work has appeared in The Rumpus, Literary Hub, Los Angeles Review of Books, The Millions, The Hairpin, The Nervous Breakdown, Minor Literatures and The Manifest-Station. She lives in Los Angeles with her husband.
